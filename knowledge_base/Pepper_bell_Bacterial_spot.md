# Pepper, bell — Bacterial spot

**Crop:** Pepper, bell  
**Condition:** Bacterial spot  
**Pathogen:** Bacterium (Xanthomonas spp.)  
**Baseline severity if untreated:** high

## Summary
Bacterial spot of pepper causes water-soaked leaf spots, defoliation, and raised scabby fruit lesions.

## Key facts
- Bacterial spot is seedborne and splash-spread; warm, wet conditions and overhead irrigation accelerate epidemics.
- Use certified clean seed/transplants, resistant varieties, copper-based bactericides, and avoid working plants when wet.

## Recommended treatment
Start with certified disease-free seed and resistant varieties; apply copper (often with mancozeb) preventively and avoid overhead irrigation.

## Economic and crop impact
Defoliation exposes fruit to sunscald and scabby lesions make peppers unmarketable, with potential for major fresh-market losses.
