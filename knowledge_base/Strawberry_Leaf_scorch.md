# Strawberry — Leaf scorch

**Crop:** Strawberry  
**Condition:** Leaf scorch  
**Pathogen:** Fungus (Diplocarpon earlianum)  
**Baseline severity if untreated:** moderate

## Summary
Leaf scorch produces dark purple blotches that merge and give leaves a scorched look, weakening plants.

## Key facts
- Leaf scorch overwinters in infected leaves and spreads via splashing rain in warm, humid weather.
- Renovate beds, remove old infected foliage, improve airflow, and apply fungicides during bloom.

## Recommended treatment
Renovate matted-row beds after harvest, remove infected leaves, improve airflow, and apply fungicides at bloom if needed.

## Economic and crop impact
Reduced vigor lowers berry size and yield, especially in perennial matted-row systems.
