# Corn (maize) — Cercospora leaf spot Gray leaf spot

**Crop:** Corn (maize)  
**Condition:** Cercospora leaf spot Gray leaf spot  
**Pathogen:** Fungus (Cercospora zeae-maydis)  
**Baseline severity if untreated:** high

## Summary
Gray leaf spot forms rectangular gray-to-tan lesions that coalesce and destroy leaf area, cutting grain fill.

## Key facts
- Gray leaf spot is favored by warm, humid conditions and continuous corn with conservation tillage that leaves infected residue.
- Resistant hybrids are the foundation of control; crop rotation, residue management, and timely fungicide at tasseling reduce losses.

## Recommended treatment
Plant resistant hybrids, rotate crops and manage residue; apply foliar fungicide (e.g. strobilurin/triazole) around tasseling if disease pressure is high.

## Economic and crop impact
Severe gray leaf spot can cut grain yield by 20-40% by reducing photosynthetic leaf area during grain fill.
