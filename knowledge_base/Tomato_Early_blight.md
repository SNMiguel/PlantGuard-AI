# Tomato — Early blight

**Crop:** Tomato  
**Condition:** Early blight  
**Pathogen:** Fungus (Alternaria solani / A. linariae)  
**Baseline severity if untreated:** moderate

## Summary
Early blight forms concentric target-spot lesions on lower leaves and can cause stem and fruit lesions.

## Key facts
- Early blight starts on older, lower leaves and progresses upward; warm wet conditions and plant stress accelerate it.
- Mulching, staking, rotation, and protectant fungicides limit spread.

## Recommended treatment
Mulch and stake plants, rotate crops, remove lower infected leaves, and apply protectant fungicides (chlorothalonil/mancozeb) on a schedule.

## Economic and crop impact
Defoliation reduces yield and exposes fruit to sunscald, cutting marketable production.
