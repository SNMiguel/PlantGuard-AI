# Apple — Black rot

**Crop:** Apple  
**Condition:** Black rot  
**Pathogen:** Fungus (Botryosphaeria obtusa)  
**Baseline severity if untreated:** moderate

## Summary
Black rot causes leaf 'frogeye' spots, cankers on limbs, and a firm brown fruit rot.

## Key facts
- Black rot (Botryosphaeria obtusa) infects fruit, leaves and bark; cankers on branches are a key inoculum source.
- Prune out cankers and dead wood, remove mummified fruit, and apply fungicides from bloom through summer.

## Recommended treatment
Prune and destroy cankers and mummified fruit; apply captan or thiophanate-methyl fungicides during the season.

## Economic and crop impact
Fruit rot and limb cankers reduce yield and can kill scaffold branches if cankers are left untreated.
