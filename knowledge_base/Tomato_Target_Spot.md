# Tomato — Target Spot

**Crop:** Tomato  
**Condition:** Target Spot  
**Pathogen:** Fungus (Corynespora cassiicola)  
**Baseline severity if untreated:** moderate

## Summary
Target spot causes necrotic leaf lesions with concentric rings plus pitted fruit lesions, common in warm humid regions.

## Key facts
- Target spot develops in warm, humid conditions with extended leaf wetness and dense canopies.
- Improve airflow, rotate crops, and apply fungicides; scout fruit as well as leaves.

## Recommended treatment
Open the canopy, rotate crops, and apply protectant/systemic fungicides; monitor fruit for lesions.

## Economic and crop impact
Both foliar and fruit lesions reduce yield and downgrade fresh-market fruit.
