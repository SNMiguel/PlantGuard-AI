# Tomato — Tomato mosaic virus

**Crop:** Tomato  
**Condition:** Tomato mosaic virus  
**Pathogen:** Virus (ToMV)  
**Baseline severity if untreated:** high

## Summary
Tomato mosaic virus causes mottled light/dark green leaves, leaf distortion, and internal fruit browning.

## Key facts
- ToMV is extremely stable and spreads mechanically via hands, tools, and contaminated debris (not primarily by insects).
- Use resistant varieties and certified seed, sanitize hands/tools, and remove infected plants; milk or disinfectant rinses reduce mechanical spread.

## Recommended treatment
Grow resistant varieties from certified seed, sanitize hands and tools, avoid tobacco use near plants, and remove infected plants. No curative treatment.

## Economic and crop impact
Mottling, distortion, and internal fruit browning reduce both yield and marketable quality.
