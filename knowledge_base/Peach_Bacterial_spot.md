# Peach — Bacterial spot

**Crop:** Peach  
**Condition:** Bacterial spot  
**Pathogen:** Bacterium (Xanthomonas arboricola pv. pruni)  
**Baseline severity if untreated:** moderate

## Summary
Bacterial spot causes angular leaf lesions, shot-holing, and sunken fruit spots in peaches.

## Key facts
- Bacterial spot is worse on sandy, exposed sites and susceptible cultivars; it spreads in warm, wet, windy weather.
- Resistant cultivars plus copper and antibiotic sprays reduce but rarely eliminate the disease.

## Recommended treatment
Plant resistant cultivars; apply copper-based sprays at low rates (to avoid phytotoxicity) plus oxytetracycline in high-pressure orchards.

## Economic and crop impact
Fruit spotting reduces fresh-market grade; severe defoliation weakens trees.
