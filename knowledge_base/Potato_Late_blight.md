# Potato — Late blight

**Crop:** Potato  
**Condition:** Late blight  
**Pathogen:** Oomycete (Phytophthora infestans)  
**Baseline severity if untreated:** severe

## Summary
Late blight — the cause of the Irish potato famine — produces fast-spreading water-soaked lesions and can destroy a field in days under cool, wet conditions.

## Key facts
- Phytophthora infestans spreads explosively in cool, wet, humid weather and can move from foliage to tubers, causing storage rot.
- Control requires resistant varieties, destruction of cull piles/volunteers, and timely protectant + systemic fungicides on a tight schedule.

## Recommended treatment
Apply protectant fungicides preventively and switch to systemic products (e.g. with mandipropamid) once conditions favor disease; destroy infected plants and cull piles.

## Economic and crop impact
Among the most economically damaging crop diseases in history; an unmanaged outbreak can cause total crop loss within a week.
