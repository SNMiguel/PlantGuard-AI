# Tomato — Septoria leaf spot

**Crop:** Tomato  
**Condition:** Septoria leaf spot  
**Pathogen:** Fungus (Septoria lycopersici)  
**Baseline severity if untreated:** moderate

## Summary
Septoria leaf spot forms many small circular spots with dark borders and gray centers, causing heavy lower-leaf defoliation.

## Key facts
- Septoria overwinters in debris and spreads by splashing water; it favors warm, wet conditions.
- Sanitation, mulching, rotation, and protectant fungicides keep it from progressing up the canopy.

## Recommended treatment
Remove infected lower leaves and debris, mulch, rotate crops, and apply protectant fungicides under wet conditions.

## Economic and crop impact
Progressive defoliation reduces yield and fruit quality through sunscald exposure.
