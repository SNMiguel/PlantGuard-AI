# Grape — Black rot

**Crop:** Grape  
**Condition:** Black rot  
**Pathogen:** Fungus (Guignardia bidwellii)  
**Baseline severity if untreated:** high

## Summary
Grape black rot rots berries into hard black mummies and spots leaves; one of the most destructive grape diseases in warm humid climates.

## Key facts
- Black rot overwinters in mummified berries and cane lesions; spring rains spread spores to new growth.
- Sanitation (removing mummies), canopy management, and a protective fungicide program from early shoot growth through veraison are essential.

## Recommended treatment
Remove mummified berries, apply fungicides (e.g. mancozeb, myclobutanil) from 1-3 inch shoots through fruit set, and open the canopy.

## Economic and crop impact
Can destroy nearly the entire crop in wet seasons if left unmanaged.
