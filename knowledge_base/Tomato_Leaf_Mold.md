# Tomato — Leaf Mold

**Crop:** Tomato  
**Condition:** Leaf Mold  
**Pathogen:** Fungus (Passalora fulva)  
**Baseline severity if untreated:** moderate

## Summary
Leaf mold causes pale yellow upper-leaf spots with olive-green velvety mold beneath, mainly in humid/greenhouse settings.

## Key facts
- Leaf mold is driven by high humidity and poor airflow, especially in greenhouses and high tunnels.
- Lower humidity with ventilation, space plants, use resistant varieties, and apply fungicides where needed.

## Recommended treatment
Reduce humidity through ventilation and spacing, grow resistant cultivars, and apply fungicides in protected culture.

## Economic and crop impact
Severe leaf mold defoliates plants in greenhouses, reducing fruit set and yield.
