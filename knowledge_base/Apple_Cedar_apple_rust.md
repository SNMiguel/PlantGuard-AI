# Apple — Cedar apple rust

**Crop:** Apple  
**Condition:** Cedar apple rust  
**Pathogen:** Fungus (Gymnosporangium juniperi-virginianae)  
**Baseline severity if untreated:** moderate

## Summary
Cedar-apple rust needs both apple and juniper hosts and produces bright orange-yellow leaf and fruit lesions.

## Key facts
- Cedar-apple rust alternates between juniper (cedar) and apple hosts; spores from cedar galls infect apple in spring.
- Plant resistant cultivars, remove nearby junipers where feasible, and apply fungicides at the pink-bud to petal-fall stages.

## Recommended treatment
Use resistant cultivars; apply fungicides (e.g. myclobutanil) from pink bud through early summer; remove nearby galls on junipers.

## Economic and crop impact
Heavy infection causes defoliation and blemished fruit, lowering marketable yield.
